Section 2 · The solution, benchmarked

Where the round should price.

Twelve design and developer-tool peers selected on open-core or agent-native positioning, revenue scale, and growth profile. Open Design's proposed 7.2× forward EV/ARR sits below the peer median (8.4×) — the discount is deliberate: it prices in pre-monetization risk on the enterprise layer while the OSS distribution and growth rate already clear the peer bar.

Selection criteria
  • Forward ARR $8M–$140M or public-market equivalent
  • Open-core, dev-tool, or agent-native product motion
  • Product-led growth with a free or OSS distribution layer
  • Priced in a Series A–C round or public listing since 2023
  • 2+ independent growth-equity term sheets on record

Section 6 · The moat, compared

Four paths, one we'd recommend pursuing first.

Option A · Status quo

Stay donation-funded, grow organically

~$140MIndicative valuation

For

  • Preserves full founder control
  • No dilution, no investor governance
  • Captures full upside if organic growth compounds alone

Against

  • No capital to build the enterprise/cloud layer
  • Closed-source competitors outspend on enterprise sales
  • Contributor burnout risk without paid core team
Option B · Recommended

$28M Series B, open core stays Apache-2.0

$180MPre-money valuation

For

  • Growth-equity step-up ~$1.6M in first-year committed pilot revenue
  • Small number of credible open-core-focused leads → tight, controllable process
  • Cleanest structure for long-tenured contributors and early backers

Against

  • Investor diligence on OSS-to-enterprise conversion adds 6–8 weeks
  • Community-trust messaging requires careful governance planning
Option C · Strategic partnership

Cloud/infra partner co-invests in the enterprise layer

$150M – $165MIndicative valuation

For

  • Unlocks distribution through the partner's enterprise channel
  • Retains the open core as a clean, independent asset

Against

  • Partnership terms & exclusivity complexity
  • Timeline dependent on the partner's own roadmap
  • Governance overhang from a single strategic investor
Option D · Bridge / extension note

Convertible note off the Series A instead of a priced round

$155M – $170MCap, indicative

For

  • Speed and certainty of execution
  • Defers priced-round dilution to a later, higher mark

Against

  • Lower proceeds than a full priced round
  • Note overhang complicates a later Series C
  • Doesn't fully fund the enterprise-layer build-out

Series B · §7The Ask09 / 10
Section 7 · The ask

$28M to build the enterprise & cloud layer.

We're running a narrow, 4-month process targeting 3–5 open-core-focused growth investors, with one contingent strategic co-investor in reserve, on a path to a signed term sheet by August 28, 2026.

A targeted process maximizes the probability of closing at the proposed $180M pre-money while limiting community-facing disclosure risk. Strategic outreach is held in reserve to discipline pricing. The founding team retains full optionality to stay donation-funded and remain fully community-governed if no term sheet clears the agreed valuation floor.
